Methane & Its Role

Methane (CH4) is a colorless, odorless, flammable gas that is the primary component of natural gas. Methane is the simplest hydrocarbon, consisting of one carbon atom and four hydrogen atoms. Methane is a powerful greenhouse gas. It is used primarily as fuel to make heat and light. It is also used to manufacture organic chemicals.

How methane is created?

Agriculture:-

Livestock (Enteric Fermentation): Cows, sheep, and other ruminants produce methane as a byproduct of digestion. In their stomachs, microorganisms break down food, releasing methane, which is then expelled by the animals.

Waste Management:-

Landfills: When organic waste decomposes anaerobically (without oxygen) in landfills, methane is generated. This process is driven by microorganisms that break down organic matter, such as food scraps and yard waste.
Wastewater Treatment: Methane can also be produced during the anaerobic treatment of wastewater, particularly in the digestion of sludge.

Fossil Fuel Extraction:-

Natural Gas and Oil Extraction: Methane is the primary component of natural gas. During the extraction, processing, and transportation of natural gas and oil, methane can leak into the atmosphere. This leakage is often referred to as "fugitive emissions."

Natural Sources:-

Wetlands: Wetlands are the largest natural source of methane. The waterlogged, anaerobic conditions in wetlands provide an ideal environment for methane-producing microorganisms.

Biomass Burning:-

Wildfires and Burning of Biomass: Burning organic material, such as wood or agricultural residue, releases methane along with other greenhouse gases.

Most Efficient Ways to Reduce Methane Emissions:

✴️Capture and Utilization: Capturing methane that would otherwise be vented or flared and using it as a fuel source (e.g., converting it to liquefied natural gas or using it to generate electricity) can significantly reduce emissions.
✴️Anaerobic Digestion of Manure: Capturing methane from manure using anaerobic digesters, which convert the methane into biogas for energy use, is another effective strategy.
✴️Landfill Gas Capture: Installing systems to capture methane from landfills and convert it into energy is highly effective. This strategy not only reduces methane emissions but also provides a renewable energy source.
✴️Dietary Supplements for Livestock: Adding methane inhibitors or feed additives like seaweed to livestock diets can drastically reduce methane emissions from enteric fermentation.
